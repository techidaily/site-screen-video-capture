## 1\. Taking Screenshots on a Chromebook with a Keyboard

Similar to the typical CTRL + PrtScn or PrtScn shortcut used on most Window‚Äôs PCs, or the Command + Control + Shift + 3 shortcut used on Mac devices, taking screenshots with the use of a keyboard on a Chromebook is equally as straight forward. To capture a screenshot of the entire screen, the process requires the use of the **Windows Switcher Key** (look for 3 layered rectangles that resemble open tabs/windows) ‚Äì this is generally located above the number 6 and 7 keys and close to the screen-brightness toggles.

![ Chromebook Screenshot](https://images.wondershare.com/filmora/article-images/chromebook-keyboard-screenshot.jpg)

The command ‚Äú**Ctrl + \*Windows Switcher Key\***‚Äù will capture a screenshot of your current page, and the command ‚Äú**Ctrl + Shift + \*Windows Switcher Key\***‚Äù will allow users to capture a partial screenshot with the help from a handy drag-selector tool.

**Note**: If your keyboard doesn‚Äôt seem to have the Screen Switcher key, **try using F5 instead**. To ensure your screenshot is copied into a specific document, select ‚Äú**Copy to Clipboard**‚Äù. From here, you‚Äôll be able to paste directly into a document.

## 2\. Taking a Screenshot on a Chromebook with the Use of Side Buttons

Using your Chromebook in tablet mode? If so, then there‚Äôs also a handy way to make use of the screenshot feature whilst on the go. Unfortunately, this feature is only useful if you‚Äôre looking to capture your entire screen; you‚Äôll need to utilize the keyboard or stylus if you‚Äôre planning to crop out a specific section.

**Note:** No matter whether you‚Äôre utilizing the Chromebook in either landscape or portrait mode, the Volume Rockers still remain in the same orientation, so it‚Äôs worth noting which button relates to ‚ÄúVolume Down‚Äù.

## Where Do I Find by Screenshots After They‚Äôre Captured?

If you‚Äôre wondering where your newly captured screenshots are disappearing to once obtained, then you need look no further than the **Downloads Section** of your **Files App.** Automatically saved, by default, in a format similar to ‚Äú**Screenshot 2012-08-03 at 5.18.34AM.png**‚Äù your recently saved PNG file can be easily renamed as you like.

Using a Guest Account? If so, then always make sure you copy across all your important screenshots to either an SD Card or Google Drive Account before you sign out. It‚Äôs important to note that the ‚ÄúDownloads‚Äù Section on a Guest Account is emptied each and every time a guest logs out.

## How Do I Edit My Screenshots on Chromebook?

Once you‚Äôve captured your screenshot and it‚Äôs been automatically saved to your downloads, there are a number of simple yet effective features built-in to help you edit your image with ease. To access your saved file, simply click on the **Launcher** (bottom left hand corner), select the **Files App Folder**, then select your recently saved screenshot within the **Downloaded Files** section.

Once you open up your captured screenshot, you should notice there are a number of icons and actions readily available. These included **editing** (pencil), the ability to **print** (printer icon), to **delete** the item (trashcan), to view it as a **thumbnail** (collage icon), and also a **slideshow** option (triangle within a square). With options such as **Auto-Fixing**, **Cropping**, **Brightness Adjustment**, and even the ability to **Rotate** your saved file, users are able to simply edit their captures at a click of a button. Great for those just looking to touch up or re-jig their saved image, there are plenty of extensions available from the **Web Store** that offer a few more alternatives (**See 4**.).
